When buying insurance, an information avalanche can overwhelm you. To protect your future from poor choices today, organize your insurance search by reaching back to grade school and employing the use of the 4 W's: Why? Who? What? When? and How much?

Why should I buy, for who, what kind, when should I buy and how much should I buy.

The classic argument to avoid life insurance runs, "If I die, why do I need money?" You don't -- but your family, your business or your favorite charity might. So anyone with dependents, dependent needs, will need life insurance.

“I have never known a widow/widower who felt there was to much life insurance in force” – anonymous
